why this exists

Sometimes you need to say it.
Not to anyone. Just to say it.

There are moments — at 2am, after the call, before the meeting, in the parking lot — when you need to say something out loud. Not to a person. Not to a journal. Not to a therapist. Just out.

Most apps would log it. Save it. Sync it. Sell it. Train on it. Show you ads about it later.

The void does none of those things. You yell. The words leave the device, into space, and disappear. There is no record. There is no account. There is no one watching.

This is what catharsis used to feel like, before everything had a database behind it.

how it works

A ritual in four steps.

No onboarding. No account creation. No friction. Open the app, say the thing, watch it go. The whole loop is under thirty seconds.

01 / OPEN

One screen, one button.

No login, no profile, no notifications. The app remembers nothing about you because there is nothing to remember.

02 / SAY IT

Type whatever you need.

No character limit. No formatting. No autosave. The dashed border quietly reminds you: this is impermanent on purpose.

03 / LAUNCH

Watch it leave.

Your words break apart, drift upward, scatter into the dark, and disappear. Three seconds. One haptic thud. Done.

04 / GONE

The void answers.

A short fragment in italics: "that one had weight." No advice. No follow-up question. Just witness, then silence.

Cosmetic upgrades. The core ritual is free, forever. If you want different "voids" to send your yells into — a volcano, a black hole, the deep ocean — you can buy them as one-time purchases through the App Store. We never see your payment information. We do not run subscriptions. We have no ads. We never sell data, because we have no data to sell.

No. Voice mode runs entirely on your device. Your microphone input is analyzed locally for amplitude — how loud, how long — and the audio data is discarded the moment you release the button. We never transcribe. We never upload. We never store. The dB meter you see runs on your phone's CPU, just like a flashlight or a level.

A small classifier runs on your device, trained to recognize a narrow set of high-signal language indicating immediate intent or active method. The bar is intentionally high — we miss many false positives rather than risk one false alarm that breaks your trust.

If it triggers, the void's voice deepens, but the choice is yours: see a real human resource, or stay in the void. Either way, your words never leave the phone.
